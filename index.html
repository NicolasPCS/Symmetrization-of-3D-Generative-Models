Symmetrization of 3D Generative Models

Nicolas Caytuiro, Iván Sipirán
Department of Computer Science - University of Chile
CENIA
arXiv Code

Overview of our proposed pipeline. The process begins with dataset preparation, where objects from ShapeNetCore.v2.PC15K are aligned and mirrored along the x = 0 plane to obtain right-side half-objects (x >= 0), each containing 15,000 points. During generation, the synthesized half-objects are mirrored across the x-axis to reconstruct full shapes, which are subsequently evaluated using the original validation set.

Abstract

We propose a novel data-centric approach to promote symmetry in 3D generative models by modifying the training data rather than the model architecture. Our method begins with an analysis of reflectional symmetry in both real-world 3D shapes and samples generated by state-of-the-art 3D generative models. We hypothesize that training a generative model exclusively on half-objects, obtained by reflecting one half of the shapes along the x=0 plane, enables the model to learn a rich distribution of partial geometries which, when reflected during generation, yield complete shapes that are both visually plausible and geometrically symmetric. To test this, we construct a new dataset of half-objects from three commonly used ShapeNet classes (Airplane, Car, and Chair) and train four 3D generative models. Experiments demonstrate that the generated shapes are symmetrical and consistent, compared with both the generated objects from the original model and the original dataset objects.

Symmetry Measurement Protocol

In order to evaluate the degree of reflection symmetry present in the 3D shape, we propose a quantitative analysis based on point cloud representations.
We analyze symmetry in current 3D shape datasets and symmetry in current 3D shape generative models.
For each point cloud and its mirrored version, we compute the Chamfer Distance. Finally, we aggregate the results into histograms.


Pipeline of our proposed Symmetry Measurement Protocol (SMP). We compute the Chamfer Distance between the original point cloud and its mirrored version to quantitatively assess the degree of reflection symmetry in 3D shapes.

Results

Once we trained four 3D generative models on the half-objects dataset, we generated new shapes and evaluated their symmetry using our proposed Symmetry Measurement Protocol (SMP). The results show that the generated shapes exhibit a high degree of reflection symmetry, as evidenced by the low Chamfer Distance values between the original and mirrored point clouds. This indicates that our data-centric approach effectively promotes symmetry in 3D generative models, leading to visually plausible and geometrically consistent shapes.
